Your Body · Your Goals
Notes specific to women at 42
🌿 Hormones & belly fat
At 42, oestrogen levels begin fluctuating, which shifts fat storage toward the abdomen. This is normal — and reversible. The core work in this program directly targets the deep transverse abdominis, which acts like a natural corset and pulls the belly wall inward. Consistency over 8–12 weeks will make a visible difference.
🍑 Why glutes matter so much
Sitting all day causes gluteal amnesia — the glutes literally forget how to fire and other muscles compensate. The Phase 1 activation work isn't easy; it's essential. Once the glutes wake up, hip thrusts and split squats will reshape the entire backside faster than almost any other exercise.
💧 Bloating vs fat
Much of what looks like belly fat in women in their 40s is actually bloating and water retention driven by stress, poor sleep, alcohol and processed foods. Reducing these alone can visibly flatten the stomach within 2 weeks — before the training even kicks in fully.
🥗 Protein is the priority
Aim for 1.4–1.8g of protein per kg of bodyweight daily. This preserves muscle mass (which drops with age), keeps you full, and supports glute growth. Greek yogurt, eggs, cottage cheese, chicken, fish and legumes are ideal sources.
😴 Sleep is not optional
Poor sleep raises cortisol, which specifically stores fat around the midsection. 7–8 hours of quality sleep will do more for your belly than an extra workout. This is especially true after 40 when the body's recovery capacity is more sleep-dependent.
📏 Measure smart
Don't rely only on the scale. Muscle is denser than fat — you may weigh the same but look and feel completely different. Take a waist measurement and a photo on Day 1, then recheck at Week 6. That's your real progress tracker.
🔁 Hip flexor stretch — daily
Tight hip flexors from sitting tilt the pelvis forward, pushing the belly out and flattening the glutes. 2 minutes of kneeling hip flexor stretch every morning will unlock better posture, more effective glute activation, and a flatter-looking belly — even before you lose a gram of fat.
